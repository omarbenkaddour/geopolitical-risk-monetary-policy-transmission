# Geopolitical Risk and Monetary Policy Transmission

[Thesis PDF](./Undergraduate%20Thesis.pdf)

Undergraduate thesis on how geopolitical risk reshapes monetary policy transmission in Egypt, Turkey, and Israel, using identified policy shocks, orthogonalized geopolitical risk shocks, and state-dependent local projections to model heterogeneous impulse responses across key macro-financial channels. Advised by Itay Goldstein at Wharton.:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Main Findings
The results show that monetary-policy transmission is state-dependent on contemporaneous geopolitical risk shocks, with the clearest evidence concentrated in financial variables tied directly to transmission channels. Several responses exhibit sign reversals across states rather than simple amplification or attenuation. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

- **Egypt:** significant state dependence appears in CDS spreads at horizons 8–10, long yields at 2–4 and 10, credit at 4–7 and 12, and prices at 2–12, with especially strong evidence that high GPR shifts the inflation response to tightening. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5} :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6} :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
- **Israel:** significant differences appear in the exchange rate at 0–4, long yields at 1–10, CDS at medium horizons, credit at short horizons, and prices at selected short and medium horizons; the exchange-rate and yield responses show especially clear sign reversals across states. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8} :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
- **Turkey:** state dependence is concentrated in the exchange rate at 1–3, long yields at 1–4, and prices at 3–10, with tightening becoming depreciationary and inflationary under high GPR. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10} :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
